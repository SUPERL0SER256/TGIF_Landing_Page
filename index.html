ABOUT US

The Global Impact Forum (TGIF) is a 501(c)3 entity that surfaces under-represented voices and populations through narrative building, and enables cross-learning between the US and the Global Majority world.

  • Disrupt the traditional one-way flow of knowledge from the Global North to the rest of the world
  • Carry narratives and thought-leadership from the majority world to the US, Europe and UK, thereby influencing global funding and programmatic practices

VISION

Knowledge is one of the most powerful levers for change, but access to high-quality, context-rich knowledge in the social sector remains fragmented and unequal.

Too often, the perspectives and lived experiences of practitioners and communities from the Global Majority are underrepresented in mainstream discourse and consequently, in decision making. This creates an information asymmetry creating fragmented access and inbuilt biases.

How do we equip changemakers globally – nonprofit leaders, youth, government officers, and community members -- with the right information at the right time, with insights that are credible and contextual?

By democratizing access to knowledge, we bridge the gap between grassroots realities and high-level policy making. Our platform ensures that the lived wisdom of local actors informs global strategies, dismantling the traditional top-down flow of information.

PROGRAMS

1. Global Knowledge Commons

IDR Answers is a pilot which currently draws upon IDR’s our repository of more than 3,000 articles and 2,300 experts. The vision is to scale it to become a public-good global knowledge platform for different thematic areas.

There will be a particular emphasis on including knowledge from organisations that capture lived experience wisdom, especially from the Global Majority in order to ensure that the narratives and insights are truly diverse, relevant and actionable.

2. South Asian Women Diaspora Narratives

This multi-year program aims to change how knowledge and power flows when it comes to South Asian diaspora giving in the US.

  • Build a narrative that centers women’s leadership in diaspora giving
  • Platform and amplify women philanthropists’ voices
  • Encourage peer learning and collaboration among the women participants
  • Create a sense of sisterhood and a trusted community of support
